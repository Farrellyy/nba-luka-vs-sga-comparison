Eight Seasons, Two Philosophies

Both players entered the league in 2018. Tracking them season by season reveals a clear divergence: Luka built his game around volume from day one, while SGA's efficiency has steadily overtaken him as his role has grown.

Scoring Volume PPG

Luka has out-scored SGA in most seasons, peaking near 34 PPG. SGA's volume has climbed steadily and now sits in the same range, but Luka has generally held the raw scoring edge.

Shooting Efficiency TS%

SGA has held a consistent edge in True Shooting%, recently climbing past 66% while Luka sits in the low-60s. This is the clearest, most consistent gap between the two players.

Luka came out of the gate with an elite PER as a teenager. SGA took longer to climb, but has now overtaken Luka in the last three seasons, reflecting both his efficiency gains and growing offensive workload in Oklahoma City.

The Verdict

Across both career trends and this season's snapshot, Luka Dončić and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander represent two distinct paths to elite offensive production. Luka generates more total volume — higher scoring, more assists, more rebounds — by carrying a heavier offensive workload. SGA gets to a comparable level of value through significantly higher efficiency, particularly his True Shooting%, relying less on volume and more on shot selection.

SGA has won back-to-back MVPs and a Finals MVP; Luka has won neither. But team context plays a real role here — the Thunder built a genuinely elite supporting cast around SGA before their title run, while Luka's prime Dallas seasons featured thinner rosters, and his Lakers tenure began mid-rebuild. Award voting tends to reward team success as much as individual production, which makes it an imperfect measure of who the better player is in isolation.

My take: Luka's elite playmaking and scoring volume make him the more valuable offensive engine — the kind of player who can instantly elevate any roster around him. That doesn't diminish SGA's career; leading a rebuild to a championship is a genuine achievement, and his efficiency edge would matter more or less depending on the roster around either player.
